The Situation

Why This Reassessment Matters More Than Any Before It

Pulaski County operates on a six-year property reassessment cycle. The last one took effect in 2021. In that time, median home prices have surged — meaning 2027 assessments will reflect six years of accumulated appreciation in a single adjustment.

Critical Understanding

The equalized rate is not a tax cut. It is the rate that keeps your actual tax bill the same despite higher assessed values. Maintaining the $0.74 rate after a large reassessment is, by definition, a tax increase — one that bypasses voters entirely. Virginia Code § 58.1-3321 ("Truth in Taxation") is your primary legal weapon.

Calendar of Action

Your Critical Window: Now Through Spring 2027

Every stage below has a specific action you should take. Missing the informal hearing forfeits your easiest avenue for relief.

Now – Fall 2026

Field Data Collection by Vision

Request your Property Record Card from the Commissioner of the Revenue. Document any errors in square footage, bedrooms, bathrooms, garage spaces, or condition. Use the county GIS Parcel Viewer at pulaskicounty.org/parcels.

November 2026

Assessment Notices Mailed

Review immediately upon receipt. Compare your new value to your Property Record Card and comparable properties. Begin assembling appeal evidence: photographs, comps, repair records.

November – December 2026

Informal Hearings with Vision

This is your fastest, cheapest, and most effective path. Contact Vision's Reassessment Office: (540) 994-2427 / reassessment@pulaskicounty.org. Bring your Property Record Card, photos, and comparable data.

Early 2027

Board of Equalization Hearings

If the informal review fails, appeal to the Board of Equalization — Circuit Court-appointed citizens with authority to increase, decrease, or affirm any assessment. Under Va. Code § 58.1-3331, you're entitled to all assessor data at least 45 days before your hearing.

Jan 1 – Apr 1, 2027

Elderly & Disabled Relief Window

Ensure all eligible neighbors apply. The relief cap is currently $300 — dramatically insufficient. Flooding this program is both a personal benefit and a political act that reduces the county's expected revenue windfall.

February – May 2027

Budget Process & Rate Decision

The Board holds public comment at every regular meeting — the fourth Monday of each month at 7 p.m. Pack the room during budget hearings. Organized, sustained presence changes political calculus. This is the decisive moment.

Challenging Your Assessment

Three Paths to Relief

Virginia law provides a clear escalating appeals process. Start at Step 1 and proceed only if necessary. The informal hearing is free; Circuit Court is not.

Factual Error Correction

Request your Property Record Card and review for wrong square footage, incorrect bedroom or bathroom counts, non-existent improvements, unrecorded deterioration, or misclassified land use. Virginia Code § 58.1-3981 gives the County Assessor authority to correct factual errors immediately. This is the fastest and cleanest path.

Informal Appeal with Vision Government Solutions

During the November/December 2026 hearing period, meet directly with Vision assessors. Bring your Property Record Card, photographs, and comparable property data from the county GIS Parcel Viewer. Vision is contractually required to consider factual evidence. This is free and preserves your options.

Board of Equalization

Three to five Circuit Court-appointed citizens. Hearings are public. Under Va. Code § 58.1-3331, you are entitled to all assessment records and data at least 45 days before your hearing. If the assessor fails to provide this within five days of your hearing, they cannot use that data against you.

Circuit Court Appeal

Under Va. Code § 58.1-3370. The most powerful and most expensive option. Property owners sharing similar grievances — such as an entire neighborhood assessed disproportionately — should consider pooling resources with a shared attorney. The threat of mass litigation has historically produced administrative concessions before cases are heard.

The Rate Battle

Where the Larger War Is Won or Lost

The assessment fight is important, but the tax rate decision is where you can protect every property owner in the county simultaneously.

"Virginia Code § 58.1-3321 requires you to adopt the equalized rate unless you hold a separate public hearing and vote to explicitly override it. I am asking this Board to adopt the mathematically derived equalized rate for tax year 2027 and to reject any budget proposal that requires a rate above that level."

Va. Code § 58.1-3321 · "Truth in Taxation"

When a general reassessment would result in an increase of 1% or more in total real property taxes collected, the county must automatically reduce the tax rate to a "revenue-neutral equalized rate" — the rate that produces no more than 101% of the prior year's collections. The Board can override this only by holding a separate public hearing (which cannot be combined with the budget hearing), publishing notice in the newspaper in a display ad no smaller than 1/8-page with an 18-point headline, and voting affirmatively. That public hearing is your primary pressure point.

Va. Code § 58.1-3253 · Annual or Biennial Reassessment

Any county may adopt annual or biennial assessments by ordinance. Demand the Board transition to biennial reassessment after 2027. This eliminates the compounding "assessment shock" of six-year cycles and requires transparent rate decisions more frequently.

When appearing before the Board, ask specifically: What is the projected annual operating cost of the Sportsplex once it opens in fall 2026, and how much will come from general funds vs. earned revenue? Has the school system maximized available state and federal grant funding before requesting additional local tax support?

Tax Relief Programs

Exemptions That Are Dramatically Underutilized

The county's existing relief programs are leaving money on the table. Flooding these programs is both a personal financial benefit and a collective political act.

Constitutionally Guaranteed

100% Disabled Veteran Exemption

Under Virginia Code § 58.1-3219.5 and Article X, Section 6-A of the Virginia Constitution, any veteran rated by the VA as 100% permanently and totally disabled pays zero real estate tax on their principal residence — regardless of income or net worth. There are no income limits and no asset caps.

  • Surviving spouses of 100% disabled veterans (if not remarried, occupying as principal residence)
  • Surviving spouses of service members killed in the line of duty (effective January 1, 2025)
  • Veterans rated less than 100% but compensated at 100% due to individual unemployability (IU), provided they also have a permanent and total rating
  • Surviving spouses of first responders (law enforcement, firefighters, EMS) killed in the line of duty
Reform Urgently Needed

Elderly & Disabled Relief — Cap Must Be Raised

Pulaski County's program is currently capped at $300 — a limit rendered almost meaningless against 2027 assessment values. In 2024, the average exemption was approximately $255, with roughly 775 qualified households. Total program cost: approximately $197,625 — less than one-tenth of one percent of annual real estate tax revenue.

  • Demand the Board raise the maximum relief cap to at least $600, doubling the current cap
  • Index the cap automatically to the percentage increase in county-wide assessed values after each reassessment
  • Raise the qualifying income threshold from $30,000 to $40,000 to reflect 2025 cost-of-living realities
  • Application window: January 1 – April 1 each year. Contact the Commissioner of Revenue
